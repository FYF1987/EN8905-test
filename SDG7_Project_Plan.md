# Project Plan: "Gas Today, Sun Tomorrow?" — SDG 7 and Bahrain's Energy Transition

**Course:** EN8905 Energy, Environment and Sustainability
**Assessment:** Individual Project/Presentation (35%)

---

## 0. One-line pitch

Bahrain currently generates most of its electricity from natural gas. This project asks: how well does that meet SDG 7 (Affordable and Clean Energy), and how would a shift toward utility-scale solar PV compare — technically, economically, environmentally, ethically and socially?

Chosen SDG: **SDG 7 – Affordable and Clean Energy** (recommended by the brief; also touches SDG 13 – Climate Action).

---

## Stage 1 — Research the chosen SDG

**What to cover:**
- SDG 7 targets: 7.1 (universal access), 7.2 (renewable energy share), 7.3 (energy efficiency), 7.a/7.b (investment & infrastructure).
- Relevant indicators: % renewable share in final energy consumption, access to electricity (%), energy intensity.
- The real regional/global energy need this addresses: Bahrain and the wider GCC rely heavily on natural gas and oil for power and desalination, driving high per-capita carbon emissions and exposure to fuel-price volatility — while the region has some of the world's highest solar irradiance, an underused resource.
- Link to recognised frameworks: UN 2030 Agenda, Bahrain's National Renewable Energy Action Plan (target: ~5% renewables by 2025, ~20% by 2035), Paris Agreement NDC commitments.

**What to collect as evidence:** 2–3 citations (IRENA, IEA, Bahrain Electricity and Water Authority/EWA, Sustainable Energy Unit Bahrain), one paragraph explaining why SDG 7 matters *here*, not just globally.

---

## Stage 2 — Assess the energy systems (current vs. future)

| | Current: Natural-gas power generation | Future alternative: Utility-scale solar PV |
|---|---|---|
| **Technical** | Reliable, dispatchable, existing grid infrastructure, high capacity factor | Intermittent (day/weather dependent), needs storage or gas backup, falling panel costs, land-intensive |
| **Economic** | Subsidised fuel costs historically low, but exposed to global gas price swings, import/production costs | High upfront capex, near-zero marginal cost after installation, rapidly falling LCOE (levelised cost of electricity) globally |
| **Environmental** | CO₂ and NOx emissions, water use for cooling, resource depletion | Low operating emissions, but embodied carbon in manufacturing, land use, panel end-of-life waste |

**What you must produce:**
- Pull real numbers where possible: Bahrain's installed generation capacity, fuel mix %, EWA renewable targets, an approximate LCOE comparison (many published IRENA/IEA figures you can cite and reference, not copy).
- A clear, justified judgement: e.g. "Given Bahrain's high solar irradiance (~2,000+ kWh/m²/year) and falling PV costs, a hybrid gas+solar system with storage would better meet SDG 7 targets over the next decade than continued reliance on gas alone — but full transition faces land and storage-cost barriers."
- This is the **highest-weighted rubric item (30/100)** — spend the most depth here.

---

## Stage 3 — Supporting practical activity

Pick **one** hands-on activity that generates your own data (not just cited figures):

**Recommended option — Mini solar output measurement:**
1. Get a small solar panel (or solar phone charger / garden light panel) + multimeter.
2. Measure voltage/current output at 3 different times of day (morning, midday, late afternoon) and note weather/cloud conditions.
3. Calculate approximate power output (W = V × I) and estimate what a rooftop-scale system would need to power a typical Bahraini household's daily consumption (~15–20 kWh/day is a reasonable estimate to cite and compare against EWA data).
4. Compare your measured real-world variability against the "current system" (gas) which doesn't have this variability — this becomes direct evidence for your technical trade-off discussion in Stage 2.

**Alternative option — Home/office energy audit:**
1. List major appliances, their rated power, and daily usage hours.
2. Estimate daily/monthly kWh consumption and compare to EWA average household consumption figures.
3. Identify where solar generation or efficiency measures could offset consumption.

Document: method, raw data/photos, calculations, and **explicitly link results back to your Stage 2 assessment** (this linkage is what the rubric rewards).

---

## Stage 4 — Ethical and social dimensions

- **Ethical framework to reference:** e.g. the UN's *Just Transition* principles, or an engineering ethics code (IEEE/Engineering Council) — discuss responsibility to reduce emissions vs. responsibility to keep energy affordable and reliable.
- **Equity of access:** Who benefits from/is burdened by each system? E.g. lower-income households may be more exposed to fuel-price volatility under gas; solar transition costs (land, subsidies) must be distributed fairly; migrant/labour communities in industrial zones near power plants face different exposure.
- **Social dimension:** Who is affected (residents near power infrastructure, future generations facing climate impacts, workers in the gas sector facing transition risk), what community need is addressed (reliable affordable power, air quality, climate resilience), and why engagement matters (public buy-in needed for any national energy transition).

---

## Stage 5 — Social awareness activity (with ethics requirements built in)

**Plan:**
1. Recruit **5–10 people** (classmates, family, neighbours — mix is fine).
2. Prepare a short 5–10 minute explanation of: what SDG 7 is, your gas-vs-solar comparison, and your practical activity results.
3. **Before the session:** get verbal or written consent to participate and to use their (anonymised) feedback as evidence. If anyone is under 18, get a parent/guardian's consent and keep them anonymous.
4. **During:** ask 3–5 quiz/discussion questions to check understanding, e.g.:
   - "What does SDG 7 aim to achieve?"
   - "Name one trade-off between gas and solar power."
   - "Why does the ethical/social side of energy matter, not just the technical side?"
5. **Evidence to collect (embed in blog/video, keep consent forms separate):**
   - Dated photos of the session
   - Participant list (first names or initials only if anonymising)
   - The questions/quiz used + summarised anonymised responses
   - A short written reflection: how well did the group understand? What would you improve?
